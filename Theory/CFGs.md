Core Phrase Structure Abbreviations
S — Sentence

The complete sentence.

S → NP VP

NP — Noun Phrase

A phrase whose head is a noun.
Examples:

Brexit

the withdrawal

the United Kingdom

VP — Verb Phrase

A phrase whose head is a verb.
Examples:

is happening

announced the decision

withdrew from the EU

PP — Prepositional Phrase

A phrase starting with a preposition.

PP → P NP


Examples:

from the EU

in the UK

AP — Adjective Phrase

A phrase whose head is an adjective.
Examples:

important

relevant to policy

Word-Level Abbreviations
Det — Determiner

Words that limit or specify nouns.
Examples:

the, a, an, this, that

N — Noun

Names of things, concepts, places.
Examples:

withdrawal, vote, country

Pro — Pronoun

Words that replace nouns.
Examples:

he, she, it, they

PN — Proper Noun

Specific names.
Examples:

Brexit, India, London

V — Verb

Action or state words.
Examples:

is, withdraw, announced

Adv — Adverb

Words modifying verbs, adjectives, or clauses.
Examples:

quickly, politically, formally

P — Preposition

Shows relationship in space, time, cause.
Examples:

in, on, from, with, about

One-line English explanation

A sentence is made of a noun phrase and a verb phrase
A noun phrase names something
A verb phrase tells what happens or what is true
A prepositional phrase adds location, time, cause, relation
An adjective phrase describes a noun
